1 · The three boundaries

Surveyed from the X2 URDF and motor specs against G1-reference motion demands. These are hard embodiment constraints — not training problems. Estimated high-dynamic fidelity ceiling: ~75–85%.
3°
shoulder adduction limit — arm-across-midline motions (common in G1 data) are infeasible
−20%
leg length (pelvis–ankle 0.602 m vs G1 0.757 m) — stride scales down proportionally
11.9 rad/s

2 · How this is used

When a reference clip "won't learn", check it against these three boundaries first to rule out embodiment infeasibility — only then debug the training recipe. The overfit-ceiling report is the complementary anchor: whatever overfit achieves is by definition inside the feasible region.
